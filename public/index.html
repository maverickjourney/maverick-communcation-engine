New MVP direction

Web-based communication first. Slack optional later.

Maverick Journey is no longer built around Slack as the core communication system or database. The product now owns the relationship through the trainer dashboard, client portal, in-app messaging, client check-ins, urgent AI alerts, AI suggested replies, and click-to-text from the trainer's real phone.

Core engine

What the Communication Engine does

This is the real-time operating system between client, trainer, AI, and the database. It captures what the client says, organizes it, creates alerts, recommends next steps, and keeps the trainer accountable.

1

Client in-app messaging

Clients message the trainer inside Maverick Journey instead of relying on Slack as the main experience.

2

Structured check-ins

Clients log meals, workouts, sleep, soreness, mood, weight, symptoms, injuries, and progress updates.

3

Urgent AI alerts

The system flags dizziness, pain, missed check-ins, low recovery, unusual patterns, or anything needing trainer review.

4

AI suggested replies

AI drafts suggested responses and workout adjustment ideas for the trainer to approve, edit, or ignore.

5

Click-to-text

The trainer can tap a button to open their real phone messaging app with a prepared message to the client.

6

Optional Slack alerts later

Slack can be added later as a notification channel, but it does not own the message history or client record.

Trainer demo flow

Example: client reports dizziness after workout

This is the moment that makes Maverick Journey different from a basic fitness app. The client message does not get buried. The system turns it into a trainer action.

Client

Sarah checks in through the client portal: “I felt dizzy after legs today and my heart rate felt weird.”

AI

Maverick AI classifies the message as a potential recovery or safety concern and pulls recent sleep, workout, and check-in context.

Database

The full message, category, risk level, timestamp, and AI summary are saved to Supabase.

Trainer

The trainer dashboard shows an urgent flag on Sarah’s profile with suggested next steps.

Sarah • Client portal

I felt dizzy after legs today and my heart rate felt weird. I had to sit down for a few minutes.

Maverick AI • internal analysis

Urgent review recommended. Possible recovery/symptom concern. Trainer should review workout intensity, hydration, sleep, and recent training load.

Trainer alert

Sarah needs follow-up before next workout. Suggested action: check symptoms, reduce intensity today, and update plan if needed.

Urgent alert examples

Alerts that create trainer accountability

Other platforms show data. Maverick Journey turns data into trainer action. The trainer is responsible for reviewing the alert, checking in, changing the workout, tracking the issue, or approving an AI suggestion.

Source of truth

Supabase/database owns the client record

The biggest architectural change is that the database owns everything important. Slack should not be the place where Maverick Journey depends on history, retention, search, client records, uploads, or AI summaries.

Slack repositioned

Slack becomes optional notifications later

Slack can still be useful for trainers who already live inside Slack. But it should only receive optional notifications like urgent alerts, daily summaries, or reminders. It should not be the MVP’s main database, main client portal, or only communication system.

Use Slack later for

Trainer urgent alerts, daily digests, reminders, and optional team notifications.

Do not use Slack for

Source of truth, message ownership, client history, uploads, or the main MVP communication experience.
